Texas railroads shaped the state's development from the 1850s onward. The first railroad in Texas, the Buffalo Bayou, Brazos and Colorado Railway, began operations in 1853, connecting Houston to nearby towns.

By the early 1900s, Texas had more railroad mileage than any other state, playing a crucial role in cattle drives, cotton transport, and connecting frontier communities.
